About Me

I am a public health professional with over 6 years of experience in health program implementation and monitoring & evaluation. Currently pursuing my MSc in Epidemiology at Prince of Songkla University, I focus on mental health among migrant workers and enjoy leveraging data-driven insights to improve public health outcomes.

I have worked with international organizations such as WHO, The Union, and PSI Myanmar, specializing in program coordination, data analysis, and community-focused health strategies. My recent interest lies in applying Generative AI to research workflows and task automation.

About the Course

Generative AI for Work & Research is an 8-week live cohort course designed to teach practical AI skills for research, automation, and communication. The program covers:

  • Using LLMs for writing, data analysis, and task automation
  • AI-powered literature review and text analysis
  • Building simple research websites and dashboards
  • Designing AI-driven presentations and visualizations
  • Capstone project showcasing AI-assisted research

This course is run by GRAPH Courses at the University of Geneva and features live workshops, personalized mentorship, and lifetime access to resources.
